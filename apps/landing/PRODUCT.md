# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Filipino BS Computer Science and BS Information Technology students (years 2–4) who participate in the disaster simulation as research subjects, and curious Minecraft-native players who discover the server. Secondary audience: faculty researchers and BFP (Bureau of Fire Protection) partners who evaluate the platform's academic validity.

Primary context: student finds the landing page via school announcement or a classmate's share. They arrive skeptical ("this is for a grade?"), read quickly, and decide whether to join — the page has one shot to make the simulation feel worth their time. Researchers arrive wanting credibility signals first, gameplay second.

## Product Purpose

BERONG SMP is a Minecraft-based disaster simulation platform for academic research into fire and earthquake safety protocols. Students experience scripted fire and earthquake simulations inside a purpose-built Minecraft world; their in-game decisions are logged and analyzed for preparedness research.

Success looks like: a student reads the landing page, understands the research context without it feeling like a government form, sees the Minecraft world as genuinely compelling, and completes enrollment. The page converts skeptics — not just already-enthusiastic players.

## Brand Personality

Playful · Immersive · Educational

The brand is a research project that runs inside a game, not a game dressed up as a research project. The Minecraft aesthetic is a real design language applied with precision — every pixel-native detail (bevel borders, dot grids, monospace labels) carries meaning. Copy is direct and specific; no edtech cheerfulness ("Learning has never been more fun!"), no government-document gravity ("In accordance with BFP Regulation 2024-03…").

Voice: a knowledgeable senior student explaining a genuinely cool thesis to a peer — confident, a little excited, not condescending.

## Anti-references

- **Kid-friendly edtech**: Duolingo, Khan Academy, Kahoot. Pastel palette, cartoon iconography, rounded-everything softness. BERONG SMP is built for university students doing research, not children doing homework drills.
- **Philippine government web portal**: DepEd, DILG, BFP.gov.ph. Low-contrast flat government aesthetic, serif-heavy official forms, badge-and-seal visual hierarchy. Even though BFP is a stakeholder, the platform's aesthetic must not resemble a compliance portal.

## Design Principles

1. **Immersion over illustration** — put the viewer inside the Minecraft world. Real screenshots, simulation footage, and coordinate data carry more weight than described features or illustrated diagrams.
2. **Rigor earns the playfulness** — academic precision (exact scenario types, scoring rubrics, research citations) is what makes the game aesthetic credible. Playful without rigorous reads as toy. Rigorous without playful reads as bureaucratic.
3. **Pixel-native, not pixel-cosplay** — the Minecraft bevel-border and dot-grid system is a real visual language, applied consistently from component to component. It is not a retro skin pasted over generic web defaults.
4. **Show the learning, not the lesson plan** — features appear through simulation context (an event log excerpt, a preparedness score, a floor-plan movement trace), not through bullet-pointed capability lists.
5. **One job per section** — each section of the page has one thing to communicate. Never let a section try to do three things at once; compression creates clarity, not sections.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Key considerations:
- Color is never the sole carrier of state (preparedness tiers use color + label + icon)
- Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements (copy widget, enrollment form, mobile nav)
- Reduced-motion alternative for all entrance animations and the mascot float
- Alt text on every image that carries content; decorative images get `alt=""`
- Ensure sufficient contrast in both dark and light themes; the green brand color (#88d982 on #0d0d0d and #2e7d32 on #f4f1ee) must clear 4.5:1 for body-sized text uses
